## 1. Project Overview

**Context:** Nigeria's economy has historically depended on both agriculture and the oil sector. Understanding the relative contributions of these sectors is essential for informed economic planning and sustainable development.

**Problem Statement:** This study investigates the comparative impact of Agricultural GDP and Oil Revenue on Nigeria's economic growth between 2000 and 2021, assessing which sector contributes more consistently to economic performance.

**Approach:** Economic data were collected from reliable secondary sources and analyzed using Excel, Python, SQL, and Power BI. Trend analysis, comparative analysis, and data visualizations were used to evaluate the relationship between Agricultural GDP, Oil Revenue, and key economic indicators.

**Outcome:** The analysis revealed that Agricultural GDP contributed more consistently to Nigeria's economic growth, while Oil Revenue exhibited significant volatility. Interactive dashboards and analytical insights were developed to support evidence-based economic recommendations.

## 2. Objectives

* **Primary Objective:** To compare the impact of Agricultural GDP and Oil Revenue on Nigeria's economic growth between 2000 and 2021.

* **Secondary Objective 1:** To analyze historical trends and patterns in Agricultural GDP and Oil Revenue over the study period.

* **Secondary Objective 2:** To evaluate the consistency and relative contribution of both sectors to Nigeria's economic performance.

* **Secondary Objective 3:** To provide evidence-based recommendations for sustainable economic growth and sectoral diversification.

> 💡 *All analyses, visualizations, and insights presented in this project were designed to address these objectives and support data-driven economic decision-making.*

## 10. Analysis & Findings

### Key Findings

* Agricultural GDP exhibited relatively stable growth throughout the study period.
* Oil Revenue experienced significant fluctuations due to changes in global oil prices and production levels.
* Agriculture contributed more consistently to economic growth than oil revenue.
* Oil remained an important revenue source but showed greater volatility.
* Economic diversification can enhance long-term economic stability.

---

## 11. Key Insights

### Insight 1: Agriculture Provides Greater Stability

Agricultural GDP maintained a more consistent contribution to economic growth compared to Oil Revenue, highlighting its importance for sustainable development.

### Insight 2: Oil Revenue Is Highly Volatile

The oil sector's contribution fluctuated significantly over the years, making the economy vulnerable to external shocks.

### Insight 3: Diversification Supports Sustainable Growth

Reducing dependence on oil and strengthening agriculture can improve economic resilience.

### Insight 4: Macroeconomic Conditions Influence Sector Performance

Inflation and exchange rate movements affect both sectors and overall economic growth.
